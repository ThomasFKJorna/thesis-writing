* Boundary points

Given a [[file:20210416111039-metric_space.org][Metric Space]] $(X,d)$ and a subset $A \subseteq X$, we say that a point $x \in X$ is called a *boundary point for $A$* if
\begin{align}
\forall \epsilon > 0 : & B_\epsilon(x) \cap A \neq \emptyset &  \text{ and}\\
          & B_\epsilon(x) \cap A^c \neq \emptyset &
    \label{eq:boundarypoint}
\end{align}
