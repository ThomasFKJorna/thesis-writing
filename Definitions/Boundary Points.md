# Boundary Points

Given a [[Metric Space]] $(X,d)$ and a subset $A \subseteq X$, we say that a point $x \in X$ is called a **boundary point for $A$** if

$$
\forall \epsilon > 0 :  B_\epsilon(x) \cap A \neq \emptyset 
$$
and
$$
           B_\epsilon(x) \cap A^c \neq \emptyset 
$$
